Nairobi apartment noise and building management tool


You are building a full production-grade building community management and dispute resolution platform for Nairobi apartment blocks. Read the full brief, expand the vision, present ~20 substantial phases, wait for "next."
The pain:
Apartment living in Kilimani, Westlands, South B, and Lavington is chaotic at the management layer. Noise complaints, parking disputes, utility bill allocations, maintenance requests, caretaker accountability, visitor management — all of this is currently handled through fragmented WhatsApp groups that mix everything together, create drama, and resolve nothing. Residents have no formal channel. Management companies have no organised system. Facilities managers are overwhelmed with voice notes. The result is simmering resentment, unresolved issues, and buildings that are poorly managed despite residents paying service charge.
What you are building:
A building management platform with a resident-facing app and a management dashboard. Residents submit issues — noise complaint, maintenance request, parking violation, visitor pre-clearance — through a clean form with photo/audio evidence attachment. Issues are timestamped, categorised, and routed to the right person (caretaker for maintenance, management for noise complaints, security for visitor access). Management sees a dashboard of all open issues, response times, and resolution status. Automated reminders go to management if an issue is unresolved after 48 hours. Residents see the status of their issue in real time. No more WhatsApp chaos.
Critical nuances:
The product is sold to building management companies (who manage tens of buildings) — not to individual buildings directly. One management company signing up = 20 buildings onboarded. The resident experience must be accessible — a WhatsApp bot version for residents who won't use a web app is important. The evidence attachment (audio recording for noise complaints, photo for maintenance) is what makes this legally useful — management can document and act on issues properly. Anonymity for noise complaints should be optional — some residents fear retaliation from neighbours. The platform should support multiple languages — Swahili and English minimum.
Expand this. Think about: a service charge payment tracker; a building notice board replacing the WhatsApp group for announcements; a visitor pre-clearance system integrated with security at the gate; a contractor job tracker for when maintenance work is commissioned; annual general meeting (AGM) management tools for residents' associations; and a building reputation score that helps prospective tenants evaluate a building before moving in.
Hosting: Next.js on Vercel. Backend on Railway. WhatsApp Business API for the bot version. PostgreSQL. Push notifications via web push. Docker-ready.
